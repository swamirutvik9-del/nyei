Founding Convenor

Message from the Founding Convenor

When I began working on NYEI, I was not trying to create a large organization. I was trying to solve one specific problem that I had seen repeatedly: young people across India — talented, determined, and capable — who had never had a single genuine encounter with the professional or institutional world.

Not because they lacked the ability. But because no one had ever opened the right door for them. And in a country as vast and diverse as India, those doors remain invisible to the majority of students.

NYEI was founded on the belief that exposure is not a privilege — it is a right that we, as educators, professionals, and institutions, have a responsibility to make available. The Guest Lecture Series, Future Skills programs, and Institutional Exposure visits are our practical answer to that responsibility.

We do this quietly, without ceremony or spectacle. We coordinate with school principals. We invite working professionals. We bring students to institutions. We measure our work by the conversations that happen and the perspectives that shift.

I invite every educator and institutional leader reading this to consider what role they might play — not just as a host, but as a committed participant in ensuring that the next generation of India's students can see, clearly, what they are building toward.

Swami Rutvik
Founding Convenor, National Youth Exposure Initiative · Ashvyn Group
About
Swami Rutvik
Swami Rutvik is the Founding Convenor of the National Youth Exposure Initiative (NYEI). He started NYEI with a clear belief — students in every region deserve access to meaningful exposure and future-ready learning. His work focuses on connecting institutions with professionals, leaders, and thinkers to ensure students gain perspectives beyond textbooks.

Background

Early Social Engagement & Community Work

Before formally structuring NYEI, various grassroots engagement efforts were undertaken, including educational support activities and student outreach initiatives. These early efforts reflect the long-term commitment toward youth development and equal access to opportunity.

Leadership Structure

How NYEI Is Led

NYEI operates through a small, coordinated leadership team that manages program design, institutional partnerships, and day-to-day operations. All leadership positions are non-commercial and mission-driven.

F
Founding Convenor

Responsible for overall vision, institutional partnerships, and strategic direction of NYEI.

C
Program Coordinators

Manage individual programs — scheduling, speaker coordination, and partner institution communication.

A
Advisory Council

Educators, researchers, and professionals who provide guidance on program design and institutional relevance. To be announced.

Opportunities

Serve as an Advisory Member

NYEI is in the process of forming an Advisory Council — a group of educators, institutional leaders, and domain professionals who can help shape the initiative's academic credibility and program quality.

Advisory membership is voluntary and non-commercial. It requires approximately two to four hours of engagement per quarter. If you are an educator, researcher, or professional who believes in NYEI's mission and would like to contribute meaningfully, we welcome your expression of interest.
