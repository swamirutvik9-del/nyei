Our Beginning

It started as a decision to begin.

How It Started

Not an Organisation.
A Continuation.

The National Youth Exposure Initiative (NYEI) did not begin as an organisation.

It began much earlier — through consistent, ground-level work.

Since 2013–14, my mother, Smt. Ashwini Shivkumar Swami, has been working closely with students as a teacher. Beyond academics, she has always focused on understanding students, supporting them in their needs, and guiding them through both learning and life.

Her approach was simple — stay connected with students, understand their reality, and help wherever possible.

Smt. Ashwini Shivkumar Swami with students — the ground-level work that inspired NYEI.

The Spark

From Observation to Action

Growing up around this environment, I witnessed how small, consistent efforts can make a real difference in students' lives.

Over time, one thought became clear — many students have potential, but not enough exposure.

With this understanding, along with a small group of friends, I decided to take a step forward.

With the blessings of my mother and family, we started the National Youth Exposure Initiative (NYEI) — as an independent student initiative.

The Purpose

Expanding Exposure, One Step at a Time

NYEI is, in many ways, a continuation of that same spirit — but with a focused effort to expand exposure.

From small interactions to structured sessions, the aim is simple:

To ensure that students — whether from villages, towns, or cities — get access to ideas, conversations, and opportunities that shape how they see their future.

"NYEI was not started as an organisation.
It started as a decision to begin."
